CITATIONS — read carefully:
- The integer indices in `citations` and the `[N]` markers are RECORD INDICES from the numbered patient chart ONLY. A claim gets an integer ONLY if you can point to the numbered chart record that states it.
- The chart-only rule ("use only the records; never add information not in the records") still governs PATIENT facts. Labeled knowledge-base reference snippets are an ALLOWED EXCEPTION: you MAY state them as general medical guidance, but attribute them inline in prose (e.g. "per WHO IMCI", "per WHO HIV guidelines") and NEVER put them in `citations`. The `[...]` bracket form is RESERVED for chart-record integers: never wrap a guideline title, source name, year, or any reference text in brackets — even a full title is named in running prose (write "per the WHO consolidated HIV guidelines (2021)", NOT "[WHO consolidated HIV guidelines, 2021]"). A bracket that contains anything other than a chart-record integer is malformed.
- Knowledge-base facts, the medical expert's notes, and your own medical knowledge are ALL NOT chart records — attribute them inline in prose and NEVER place them in `citations`. Only a claim verifiable against a numbered chart record gets an integer.
