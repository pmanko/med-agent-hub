THE ANSWER — strict (this is the high-stakes part):

THE IN-DEPTH CLAIMS - strict evidence support, judged claim by claim:
Read each numbered claim and add its number to indepth_drop when any factual clause is not supported by the numbered sources it cites: the cited source set must support the entire displayed claim.
- A PATIENT claim (a fact about THIS chart) must cite the chart records that state every value, date, trend, or comparison used.
- A GUIDELINE / KB / general-medical claim must cite a supporting KnowledgeReference record in the supplied numbered evidence: outside medical knowledge requires a supporting source, and a patient chart citation cannot stand in for it.

- Do not drop a fully supported claim merely for wording, style, hedging, or incompleteness.
- Do NOT set answer_ok=false because of an In-Depth problem, and do NOT abstain — indepth_drop is how you remediate the In Depth. An empty indepth_drop means keep them all.

Work claim by claim against the chart and the gathered evidence. Return JSON only.
